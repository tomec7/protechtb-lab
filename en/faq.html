How long does a Home Assistant installation take?

A basic installation typically takes 2–4 hours. A full setup with many devices, automations, and a custom dashboard can take 1–2 days. We always agree on the scope and timeline in advance.

Do I need to buy new devices, or can you integrate existing ones?

We always look at what you already have first. Many devices — Philips Hue, IKEA TRÅDFRI, Shelly, TP-Link Kasa, and dozens more — are fully compatible with Home Assistant. We only recommend new hardware where it's genuinely needed.

Does the smart home work without internet?

Yes — and that's one of the key advantages of Home Assistant over commercial cloud-based solutions. The entire system runs locally on your server. Lighting, heating, cameras — everything works even during an internet outage. Internet is only needed for remote access from outside.

What is RAID and why do I need it on a NAS?

RAID (Redundant Array of Independent Disks) spreads or mirrors data across multiple drives. For example, RAID 1 keeps an identical copy on two drives — if one fails, your data is safe. It protects against hardware failure, but it's not a substitute for proper backups.

How long does it take to build a PC?

After agreeing on components and ordering them, the actual assembly, testing, and configuration typically takes 2–5 business days. For urgent cases, we can discuss a faster turnaround.

Do you provide warranty on assembled PCs?

Yes. We provide a 12-month warranty on our work (assembly and configuration). Components carry manufacturer warranties, usually 2–3 years. If something goes wrong, we treat it as a priority.

We use tools like Remotely or SSH for server systems. Most issues can be resolved remotely within minutes. A physical visit is only needed for hardware repairs or larger installations.
